About

This is a FICTIONAL Character for the incoming Incorporeal LudoNarratives setting Fixed Cards. These character profiles are based on normative stereotypes of gender and sexuality and are the beginning of your card game and RPG journey. The purpose of these ludonarratives is to question normativity, which we understand to be the real problem. Being normative means rejecting differences and human diversity, believing that everyone should behave according to a norm. You can be cis, trans, homo, straight, bi, it doesn't matter. Just don't think everyone has to be like you! We hope you live your ludonarrative journey and then post it here.

Beauty and the Beast

In fashion fetishism, certain garments create, in a kind of ritual, a relation between sex and domination. The elements of this type of clothing always refer to domination, whether through punishment, discipline, or the game of hide and reveal: underwear (panties and bras), catsuits (one-piece tights), corsets and bondage (bindings), seven-eighth stockings, high-heeled shoes and boots, and the second skin, adherent materials such as leather and rubber, or transparent like silk and satin, in addition to the tattoo and piercing itself, are considered as fetish clothing. All this game of dominance is still associated with a nostalgic time when prostitution was considered some sort of “way out” for rebellious women, who would escape marriage or cloister in favor of an allegedly free life. STEELE, V. Fetiche: moda, sexo e poder. Cidade: Editora, 1997.
