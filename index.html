About

This is a FICTIONAL Character for the incoming Incorporeal LudoNarratives setting Fixed Cards. These character profiles are based on normative stereotypes of gender and sexuality and are the beginning of your card game and RPG journey. The purpose of these ludonarratives is to question normativity, which we understand to be the real problem. Being normative means rejecting differences and human diversity, believing that everyone should behave according to a norm. You can be cis, trans, homo, straight, bi, it doesn't matter. Just don't think everyone has to be like you! We hope you live your ludonarrative journey and then post it here.

Beauty and the Beast

In fashion fetishism, certain garments create, in a kind of ritual, a relation between sex and domination. The elements of this type of clothing always refer to domination, whether through punishment, discipline, or the game of hide and reveal: underwear (panties and bras), catsuits (one-piece tights), corsets and bondage (bindings), seven-eighth stockings, high-heeled shoes and boots, and the second skin, adherent materials such as leather and rubber, or transparent like silk and satin, in addition to the tattoo and piercing itself, are considered as fetish clothing. All this game of dominance is still associated with a nostalgic time when prostitution was considered some sort of “way out” for rebellious women, who would escape marriage or cloister in favor of an allegedly free life. STEELE, V. Fetiche: moda, sexo e poder. Cidade: Editora, 1997.

The Mother Bride

Every primitive people has the same beginning […]. Man hunts and fights. The woman trains herself, she imagines: she conceives dreams and gods. She is a seer one day: she has the infinite wing of desire and dreams. To better count the days, she watches the sky. But land is no less valuable. Resting her eyes on the amorous flowers, she, who is also young and blossoming, extracts a personal knowledge from them. Woman, she asks them to heal the ones she loves. Simple and moving beginning of religions and sciences! Later, everything will be divided: we will see the appearance of the special man, conjurer, astrologer and prophet, necromancer, priest, doctor. But in the beginning the woman is everything. MICHELET, 1862. The sorceress. Introduction (p. 321). Apud BARTHES, R. Michelet eater of History. São Paulo: Editora Schwarcz, 1991, p. 140.

The Femme Fatale

Lilith, created together with Adam, and who is not mentioned in most Catholic bibles, is the first woman in creation, expelled from paradise and identified with Evil and the serpent; Eve's sister, both in origin and in the dispute over the first Adam. Lilith is amoral, lewd, and unruly, as opposed to Eve's warmth, kindness, and submissiveness. Lilith is associated with lunar myths, according to the tradition of identifying the Moon with feminine principles. The same central symbolism of fecundity and regeneration submitted to the Moon and distributed by the star itself or by substantial forms ( Magna Mater, Terra Mater) explains the presence of the serpent in the iconography or in the rites of the great goddesses of universal fertility. ELIADE, Apud: CONTRERA, pp. 77-78. CONTRERA, M. S. The presence of archaic content in the media. Porto Alegre: Editora Annablume, 2000.
